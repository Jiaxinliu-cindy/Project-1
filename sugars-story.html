Sugar's Story

Dogs have been a huge part of my entire family. I got my first puppy in Beijing when I was in sixth grade. After I moved to the U.S. for high school, my father moved to Southern California by himself and my mother decided to stay in Beijing. It was the beginning of my family’s separation. Two countries, three time zones. Communication and keeping each other, the two simplest things for families, became the most difficult things for us to accomplish. Because of that, my Dad brought home two new family members one day and named them Sugar and Ginger. That day, our family group chat kept on ringing as my Dad would send multiple pictures every hour. I could tell, he was excited.

Sugar and Ginger were about three months old when they first came to our house. They were about the size of my Dad’s hand. My Dad said he would take Sugar and Ginger in his jacket’s pockets when he goes out for grocery shopping. Because of Sugar and Ginger, my dad would always be the center of attention in stores as people would always be surprised by the fact that they are actually real. It was my high school junior year’s spring break when I first met Sugar and Ginger. With their company, the house was filled with laughter and happiness every day. One of Sugar’s favorite activity was chasing and barking at my Dad’s golf clubs. She would also put on a show of the dog version of the tug of war with Ginger when they fight for toys. When it was sunny outside, they enjoyed walking alongside the beach with my Dad every afternoon and would always wait for their small treats when they get home to end the day.

Sugar was the sweetest dog. She kept my Dad during his ups and downs. She had a signature pair of round and watery eyes that always had something to say. She could be a little silly sometimes so we would call her Xiao Sha Zi or Xiao Hanhan (小傻子，小憨憨). She had a curious mind and loved to explore the surroundings around her. She was loved and protected by everyone in the family and she would always greet guests and friends with her kindness and innocence.

Sugar went through a tough life filled with illnesses but she always stayed strong and hopeful. It was one afternoon that I received a text from my Dad during my Squash practice. He said that Sugar was paralyzed because an extra bone grew out on her neck and affected her nerves. The vet told us it was very unlikely that she would recover fully. Even with surgery, she lower half of the body would still be paralyzed. That day, it was the first that I heard my Dad cried in years. He struggled so much because Sugar was so young and he did not want to see her go through so much pain for the rest of her life, but at the same time, it kills him to even think about sending her away with euthanasia. After trying everything we could, my Dad decided to not go through with the surgery and started to consider euthanasia. But a miracle happened. Right after we gave up on surgery, the vet gave Sugar a hormone medicine. He was not positive that it would change anything but would not hurt to give a last try. One day after the medicine, Sugar was able to stand up and walk slowly.

We always say, “you never know which one comes first, tomorrow or accidents.” Around 7 pm, April 29th. Sugar was attacked by a coyote when she was playing with Ginger in our backyard. We got lucky that the coyote actually dropped her on the ground due to their fear of humans. My parents wrapped Sugar’s wounds immediately and drove to emergency care within 20 minutes. My Mom said that Sugar stayed strong with her eyes open in her arms. My parents called me after handing Sugar to the vet. I felt like my entire world collapsed after hearing the news. I prayed and wished for a miracle to happen but did not get any other calls from my parents for the next half an hour. I knew exactly what happened. More than an hour later, I received a text from my Mom, “Sugar left us.” That night, I cried until I fell asleep.
